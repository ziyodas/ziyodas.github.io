Homely Homecrest

My essay explores the enchanting neighborhood of Homecrest where I spent a significant part of my childhood. It's a place defined by vibrant Avenue U stores, each offering a unique slice of life from bustling 99 cent shops to the aroma-filled Chinese markets.

Walking through Homecrest's suburban blocks, I cherished witnessing elderly couples walking to the piers, bread in hand for the white swans. This neighborhood taught me independence when I first walked home alone in fifth grade, opening my eyes to its hidden gems like the lively Grand Car Wash and the pharmacy bustling with students.

Amidst these familiar sights, a new bright blue "modern" apartment building rised. Initially, I resented its presence on my daily path, feeling its fancy deameanor clashing with Homecrest's cozy, welcoming vibe. It symbolized an exclusivity foreign feeling to my immigrant family's experience, challenging my connection to the neighborhood.

Over time, however, I realized my neighborhood's true richness lies in its community spirit and cherished spots like the local sushi joint and the warm Homecrest library.
